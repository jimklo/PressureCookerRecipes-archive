I WANT to love the Cuisinart Pressure Cooker!, January 12, 2009 I've never used a pressure cooker before, and I was so excited to get my Cuisinart CPC-600 pressure cooker! At first it worked just great and I was delighted to turn out wonderful stews in 20 minutes and even meatloaf in 25 minutes. But after about a week it stopped coming up to pressure and steam poured out of the red float valve. After reading another reviewer's comments, I started tapping on the red float through the plastic cover and jarred it loose; at that point it would rise and seal the unit so it would come up to pressure. Kind of a pain that I have to do that each time.

Except for that detail, it has worked very well.

Another reviewer asked what PSI the unit works at. Very odd that they do not include this info in their user manual or web site since you need the info to adjust cooking times when using other pressure cookers' recipes, but I emailed their tech support to ask and received this answer:

low pressure - 6 PSI
high pressure - 10 PSI

Steven, I believe the psi is 15 lbs for high pressure and 10 lbs for low pressure. I cooked the Italian sausage recipe I posted the other day in it using high pressure and I reduced the time to see if it would cook at the lower time and it did. The timing charts and recipes in the manual seem to indicate that these pressures are 15 and 10 lbs. I am sure you will do fine with using standard pc recipe pressures and times with this unit.

I usually recommend storing a pc with the lid off and upside down. This prevents the gasket from being squished and also prevents mildew or mold, or odors from being in the cookbase. This cooker stores nicely with the lid upside down. I just want to caution you not to place anything on top of the lid as this has a thin sealing ring cover that could bend if you put something on it.
