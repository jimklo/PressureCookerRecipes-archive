Butternut Harvest Bisque

A smooth and mellow soup featuring a subtle blending of the fruits of autumn
and winter squash.  If Winesap apples are unavailable, substitute Rome Beauty
or another tart, flavorful variety.

2 lg butternut squash (about 6 pounds)
5 Tbls butter
2 lg onions, coarsely chopped
5 lg Winesap apples, peeled, cored and cut into eighths
3 lg ripe Bartlett pears, peeled, cored and cut into chunks
14 cups chicken broth  (112 oz)
1/2 tsp dried thyme leaves
1/4 tsp dried sage leaves
1 tsp whole black peppercorns
2 lg bay leaves
1/4 tsp ground nutmeg
6 Tbls cream sherry
1/4 tsp white pepper, preferably freshly ground
4 Tbls very finely chopped walnuts

Using a sharp knife, cut squash in half lengthwise.  Scoop out seeds & pulp &
discard.  Remove rind with vegetable peeler.  Cut flesh into 1 1/2" chunks.

Melt butter in lg pot over med-hi heat.  Add onion & cook, stirring, for 3-4
min or til limp.  Stir in squash, apples, pears and stock.  Combine the
thyme, sage, peppercorns and bay leaves in small muslin bag or cheesecloth
bag (or use metal tea ball).  Tie bag closed with string.  Add to pot along
with the nutmeg.

Bring to a boil over med-hi heat.  Lower ht & simmer, covered, for about 20
minutes or til squash is tender.  Remove spice bag.  Puree.

Stir in the cream and sherry. (Soup should be fairly thin but still have some
body.)  Add s&p.  Sprinkle each serving with some nuts.

Makes 14 - 16 servings.
